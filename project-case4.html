Marketing Campaign Analysis

ROI Optimization Through Data-Driven Customer Segmentation and Targeting

Marketing Campaign Analysis — Full Case Study Storytelling

Problem Identified

The marketing team could not demonstrate campaign ROI or identify the highest-value audience segments. Key challenges included:

  • Unclear return on marketing spend
  • Inefficient targeting across customer segments
  • Poor attribution of conversions and channel contribution
  • Difficulty identifying campaign winners and losers
  • Limited insight into customer behavior and lifetime value

Without a unified view, leadership was making investment decisions based on partial data and assumptions.

My Role & Approach

I built an analytics framework and Power BI dashboard that turned campaign data into actionable marketing intelligence.

a. Data Preparation

  • Integrated campaign, customer, sales, and channel datasets.
  • Standardized KPIs for spend, conversions, and revenue.
  • Created calculated metrics for CAC, LTV, conversion rate, and campaign ROI.

b. Exploratory Analysis

  • Identified high-value customer segments and their performance drivers.
  • Compared campaign effectiveness by channel and segment.
  • Analyzed funnel drop-off points and conversion gaps.

c. Dashboard Development

The dashboard included:

  • Executive KPI cards for ROI, CAC, revenue, and conversions.
  • Attribution analysis across channels and campaign touchpoints.
  • Funnel and conversion tracking for campaign performance.
  • Segment performance views that linked customer behavior to revenue impact.

d. Insight Storytelling

I converted campaign data into clear, prioritized recommendations for marketing, budget, and growth.

Power BI Excel Analytics SQL Server Customer Analytics Attribution

Key Insights Uncovered

  1. Top campaigns were not always most profitable. Some high-spending channels generated volume but delivered low ROI.
  2. High-value segments drove the best returns. Winners were identifiable by purchase behavior, not just reach.
  3. Attribution gaps hid underperforming spend. Better tracking revealed channels that added little incremental value.
  4. Funnel drop-offs limited conversion. Optimizing the mid-funnel delivered measurable gains.
  5. Budget shifts could unlock growth. Redirecting spend to top-performing segments increased expected revenue and lowered CAC.

Solution Impact

  • Provided a single source of truth for campaign performance.
  • Enabled marketing leadership to prioritize high-value segments.
  • Reduced wasted spend by identifying low-performing channels.
  • Improved collaboration between analytics, marketing, and finance.
  • Turned campaign data into a repeatable decision process.

Results

  • Improved marketing ROI by 156%.
  • Reduced customer acquisition cost by 35%.
  • Increased conversion rates by 3.2x.
  • Generated 42% more revenue from the same budget.
  • Enabled a $2.1M budget shift to the best-performing segments.

This dashboard made campaign performance transparent and helped the team move from intuition-based decisions to data-driven marketing strategies.
