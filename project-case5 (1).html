01 — Challenge

The Problem

Amazon marketplace reporting was fragmented, making it difficult to understand which products, channels, and fulfilment paths were truly profitable.

  • Sales, buy box, and advertising metrics were in separate, unconnected reports
  • Profitability and margin signals were unclear at the individual SKU level
  • Customer behaviour and conversion performance were difficult to connect to revenue outcomes
  • Fulfilment issues were silently impacting buy box health and sales continuity

Core issue: Without a consolidated view, the team was making pricing, inventory, and advertising decisions based on isolated, partial data — optimising one metric while inadvertently damaging another.

02 — Approach

My Role & Approach

I built an Amazon analytics dashboard that consolidated marketplace data and surfaced the most important sales and margin signals in one clear, actionable format.

a. Data Preparation

  • Combined sales, advertising, buy box, inventory, and fulfilment datasets from Amazon Seller Central
  • Standardised product identifiers, channel names, and date dimensions across all sources
  • Created calculated metrics for margin contribution, buy box share, and conversion performance

b. Exploratory Analysis

  • Identified which SKUs were high-revenue but low-margin — a common but costly blind spot
  • Analysed buy box win rates and correlated them with listing health indicators
  • Reviewed advertising spend efficiency against actual conversion and net margin contribution

c. Dashboard Development

  • Revenue & Margin View — performance by SKU and category, with margin clearly surfaced
  • Buy Box Health Monitor — real-time listing quality and win rate tracking
  • Sales Trend Analysis — channel and customer segment breakdowns over time
  • Fulfilment & Inventory Impact — stock availability and its effect on sales continuity
  • Advertising Efficiency Report — spend vs. conversion vs. margin contribution

d. Insight Storytelling

I turned the dashboard into a narrative that clarified where the business should invest, optimise, and improve — connecting each data point directly to a commercial decision.

03 — Insights

Key Insights Uncovered

  1. High sales did not equal high margin. Several top-selling SKUs generated strong revenue but poor margin contribution — they were being promoted at a hidden cost to overall profitability.
  2. Buy box health was a leading performance signal. Listings with low buy box share consistently underperformed on conversion, even when traffic was healthy — a metric that had been largely invisible before this project.
  3. Advertising spend had mixed and uneven returns. Some campaigns drove volume but generated little profitable revenue after fees and cost of goods — growing sales, not the business.
  4. Fulfilment risk was a silent revenue disruptor. Poor inventory visibility created stock-outs that directly cost buy box share — a chain reaction starting in the warehouse and ending in lost sales and rankings.
04 — Impact

Solution Impact

  • Centralised all Amazon performance reporting into a single, integrated dashboard
  • Made profitability visible at the SKU level for the first time — shifting focus from revenue to margin
  • Reduced reporting friction and improved operational alignment across teams
  • Enabled faster, evidence-based decisions on pricing, inventory management, and ad spend
  • Established a reliable analytics foundation for ongoing marketplace optimisation
