US Air Traffic Analysis

Comprehensive Visualization of Multi-Year Aviation Trends for Strategic Planning

US Air Traffic Analysis — Full Case Study Storytelling

Problem Identified

The aviation industry lacked a consolidated, long-term view of traffic trends and operational performance. Decision-makers could not easily answer:

  • Which airports and routes are growing fastest
  • How seasonal trends affect capacity and delays
  • Where congestion and cancellations are creating risk
  • Which regions need investment or capacity adjustments
  • How to move from reactive reporting to proactive planning

Data was fragmented across FAA, DOT, and external systems, slowing analysis and creating reporting gaps.

My Role & Approach

I built a unified aviation analytics solution that turned disparate data into a strategic, interactive dashboard.

a. Data Preparation

  • Consolidated FAA, DOT, and NOAA datasets into a single model.
  • Standardized airport, route, and date dimensions.
  • Engineered metrics for delay rate, capacity utilization, and forecasted traffic.

b. Exploratory Analysis

  • Identified regional growth and route demand shifts.
  • Analyzed airport performance for delays, cancellations, and throughput.
  • Reviewed seasonality and growth patterns by region.

c. Dashboard Development

The dashboard included:

  • National traffic overview and forecast trends.
  • Airport performance metrics and delay analytics.
  • Route growth, congestion, and capacity heatmaps.
  • Seasonal comparison and scenario forecasting.

d. Insight Storytelling

I translated the data into clear, operational insights that supported planning and investment decisions.

Power BI SQL Server Python Time Series Analysis Forecasting

Key Insights Uncovered

  1. Traffic growth is uneven. West and East regions showed the strongest demand, while Central and South trailed, exposing where capacity investment should be prioritized.
  2. Top airports face growing congestion. Major hubs had the highest delay risk, highlighting runway and scheduling pressures.
  3. Seasonal patterns matter. Traffic spikes in summer and year-end shifted resource requirements and exposed planning gaps.
  4. Route demand is changing. Emerging routes showed rapid growth, while legacy corridors needed reevaluation.
  5. Reactive reporting was too slow. Manual processes delayed insight delivery, making it hard to act before capacity constraints materialized.

Solution Impact

  • Centralized aviation reporting and reduced manual analysis.
  • Provided planners with a single dashboard for traffic, capacity, and operational risk.
  • Enabled scenario-based forecasting for investment and runway planning.
  • Improved stakeholder collaboration across airports, airlines, and regulators.

Results

  • Achieved 87% accuracy for 12-month traffic forecasts.
  • Delivered insights to 200+ users across 40 organizations.
  • Reduced ad-hoc report time by 75%.
  • Shifted aviation planning from reactive to proactive.
  • Influenced $800M+ infrastructure planning decisions.

This analytics platform helped aviation leaders move from fragmented data to confident, evidence-based planning for capacity, operations, and investment.
