01 — Challenge

The Problem

The aviation industry lacked a consolidated, long-term view of traffic trends and operational performance. Decision-makers across airports, airlines, and regulatory bodies were flying blind on strategic planning questions.

  • Which airports and routes were growing fastest — and which were declining
  • How seasonal patterns affected capacity requirements and delay risk
  • Where congestion and cancellations were creating systemic operational risk
  • Which regions needed investment or infrastructure capacity adjustments
  • How to shift from reactive reporting to forward-looking planning

Root cause: Data was fragmented across FAA, DOT, and external systems — slowing analysis, creating reporting gaps, and leaving planners unable to act before capacity constraints materialised.

02 — Approach

My Role & Approach

I built a unified aviation analytics solution that consolidated disparate data sources into one strategic, interactive dashboard — enabling planners to see the full picture and act with confidence.

a. Data Preparation

  • Consolidated FAA, DOT, and NOAA datasets into a single unified data model
  • Standardised airport codes, route identifiers, and date dimensions across all sources
  • Engineered custom metrics for delay rate, capacity utilisation, and forecasted traffic volumes

b. Exploratory Analysis

  • Identified regional growth corridors and shifting route demand patterns
  • Analysed airport-level performance across delays, cancellations, and passenger throughput
  • Reviewed multi-year seasonality and traffic growth patterns by region and airline

c. Dashboard Development

  • National Traffic Overview — total passenger volumes, trends, and year-over-year growth
  • Airport Performance Metrics — delay analytics, cancellation rates, and throughput by hub
  • Route Growth & Congestion Heatmaps — visual identification of capacity pressure points
  • Seasonal Comparison Module — demand spikes, staffing gaps, and planning windows
  • Scenario Forecasting Tool — 12-month and multi-year traffic projections by region

d. Insight Storytelling

I translated raw aviation data into clear, prioritised operational narratives that planning teams could act on — connecting data findings directly to infrastructure investment and capacity decisions.

03 — Insights

Key Insights Uncovered

  1. Traffic growth was highly uneven. West and East regions showed the strongest sustained demand, while Central and South significantly trailed — exposing where capacity investment should be concentrated.
  2. Major hubs faced growing congestion risk. Top-performing airports had the highest delay rates, signalling runway scheduling pressures that would compound without proactive intervention.
  3. Seasonal patterns exposed planning gaps. Summer and year-end traffic spikes consistently shifted resource requirements in ways that manual planning had failed to anticipate or prepare for.
  4. Route demand was fundamentally changing. Emerging short-haul and regional routes showed rapid growth, while some legacy long-haul corridors required complete strategic reevaluation.
  5. Reactive reporting was too slow to be useful. Manual analysis delayed insight delivery by days or weeks — making it impossible to act before capacity issues became operational crises.
04 — Impact

Solution Impact

  • Centralised aviation reporting and eliminated manual, fragmented analysis workflows
  • Gave planners a single dashboard covering traffic, capacity, and operational risk in one view
  • Enabled scenario-based forecasting for runway investment and infrastructure planning cycles
  • Improved stakeholder alignment across airports, airlines, and regulatory bodies
  • Established a repeatable, scalable analytics foundation for future planning rounds
