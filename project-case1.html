01 — Challenge

The Problem

The retail business was generating millions in sales across the United States, yet leadership had no clear, unified view of performance. Data lived in scattered spreadsheets, making it nearly impossible to answer critical business questions quickly.

  • Which regions and cities were truly driving growth
  • Whether high sales were actually translating into high profit
  • Which product categories were quietly eroding margins
  • How discounting behaviour was impacting overall profitability
  • Where to focus marketing, inventory, and pricing efforts

Core issue: Without a centralised view, decisions were reactive, slow, and based on assumptions rather than evidence. The organisation needed a single source of truth.

02 — Approach

My Role & Approach

I designed and developed an interactive Retail Performance Dashboard that transformed fragmented data into a strategic decision-making tool, following a structured analytics workflow from raw data to executive insight.

a. Data Preparation

  • Consolidated sales, profit, discount, and regional datasets from multiple sources
  • Cleaned inconsistencies and standardised all formats for reliable analysis
  • Engineered custom metrics: Profit Margin, Predicted Profit, and Discount Impact

b. Exploratory Data Analysis

  • Regional performance breakdown by sales and profit contribution
  • Category and sub-category profitability at the SKU level
  • Discount behaviour patterns across product lines and regions
  • City-level revenue concentration and geographic spread

c. Dashboard Development

Built a multi-layered Power BI dashboard featuring:

  • Executive KPI Cards — Total Sales $2.30M · Total Profit $286.40K · Avg. Discount 16%
  • Scenario Analysis Tool — demonstrated how a –0.53 discount change raised predicted profit to $1.50M
  • Top 10 Cities by Sales — highlighted New York, Los Angeles, Seattle, and San Francisco
  • Regional Sales Comparison — West and East identified as top performers
  • Sub-Category Profitability Table — surfaced high-margin and loss-making product lines
  • Category Trend Visualisation — compared Technology, Furniture, and Office Supplies
  • Geographic Map — visualised sales and profit distribution across all U.S. states
03 — Insights

Key Insights Uncovered

  1. High Sales ≠ High Profit Margin. Chairs generated $328K in sales but only a 3.02% margin, while envelopes, furnishings, and fasteners delivered 31–44% margins on far lower revenue — exposing where profitability was actually hiding.
  2. Over-discounting was destroying profit. The scenario tool proved that a –0.53 discount reduction raised predicted profit to $1.50M — the business was leaving significant money on the table.
  3. Regional performance was highly uneven. West and East generated $0.4M–$0.8M in sales, while Central and South significantly lagged — signalling where to invest marketing and supply chain resources.
  4. Revenue was concentrated in very few cities. New York, Los Angeles, Seattle, and San Francisco drove a disproportionate share of revenue, supporting targeted city strategies over broad national campaigns.
  5. Category trends varied sharply. Technology led in sales but had fluctuating margins. Furniture was inconsistent due to loss-making sub-categories. Office Supplies delivered the most stable margins.
  6. Hidden opportunities in sub-categories. High-margin product lines were under-prioritised. Expanding these and reducing low-margin SKUs represented a clear, actionable growth path.
04 — Impact

Solution Impact

  • Centralised reporting reduced analysis time from hours to minutes
  • Leadership gained real-time visibility into sales, profit, discounts, and regional performance
  • Pricing and discount strategies became fully evidence-based
  • Inventory decisions aligned with profitability, not just revenue volume
  • Teams collaborated better around one shared, interactive tool
  • The business shifted from reactive reporting to proactive strategy
