Case Management System Analysis

Defining Requirements and Workflows for a Centralized Support Solution

The Problem

The support team was operating with fragmented systems and manual processes to manage case information. There was no centralized platform to track case status, customer interactions, or resolution timelines. This resulted in:

  • Lack of Visibility: Stakeholders could not easily view the status of active cases or prioritize workload
  • Inefficient Workflows: Support staff spent significant time searching for information across multiple systems and spreadsheets
  • Poor Customer Experience: Customers had inconsistent support experiences and unclear timelines for resolution
  • Data Inconsistencies: No single source of truth led to duplicate records and incomplete information
  • Compliance Risks: Lack of audit trails and documentation for regulatory requirements

My Solution

Requirements Analysis & Workshops

I conducted comprehensive interviews and workshops with support teams to understand current workflows and pain points:

  • Facilitated 8+ stakeholder workshops to map existing processes
  • Identified key requirements for case tracking, notification, and reporting
  • Documented business rules and decision points

System Design & Configuration

We selected Dynamics 365 as the platform and I led the configuration:

  • Case Entity Design: Structured case records with custom fields for priority, urgency, and SLA tracking
  • Workflow Automation: Automated case routing based on severity and team specialization
  • Dashboard Creation: Built real-time dashboards for team leads and management
  • Integration Planning: Mapped integrations with email and external communication systems

Documentation & Training

Created comprehensive documentation and trained the support team on the new system.
