Amazon E-commerce Analytics — Full Case Study Storytelling

Problem Identified

Amazon marketplace reporting was fragmented, making it difficult to understand which products, channels, and fulfillment paths were truly profitable.

  • Sales, buy box, and advertising metrics were in separate reports.
  • Profitability and margin signals were unclear at the SKU level.
  • Customer behavior and conversion performance were difficult to connect.
  • Fulfillment issues were impacting sales success without clear visibility.

The organization needed a consolidated view to align sales, margin, and operational performance.

My Role & Approach

I built an Amazon analytics dashboard that consolidated marketplace data and surfaced the most important sales and margin signals.

a. Data Preparation

  • Combined sales, advertising, buy box, and fulfillment datasets.
  • Standardized product and channel identifiers.
  • Created metrics for margin contribution, buy box share, and conversion performance.

b. Exploratory Analysis

  • Identified which SKUs were high revenue but low margin.
  • Analyzed buy box performance and listing health.
  • Reviewed advertising spend against conversion and profit.

c. Dashboard Development

The dashboard included:

  • Revenue and margin performance by SKU and category.
  • Buy box health and listing quality indicators.
  • Sales trend analysis by channel and customer segment.
  • Fulfillment and inventory impact on revenue continuity.

d. Insight Storytelling

I turned the dashboard into a narrative that clarified where the business should invest, optimize, and improve.

Power BI Excel SQL E-commerce Analytics Customer Insights

Key Insights Uncovered

  1. High sales did not always equal high margin. Some top-selling SKUs generated strong revenue but low profitability.
  2. Buy box health was a leading signal. Listings with low buy box share often lagged in conversion despite decent traffic.
  3. Advertising spend had mixed returns. Some channels drove volume but not profitable sales.
  4. Fulfillment risk affected revenue continuity. Poor inventory visibility created missed opportunities and lost buy box share.

Solution Impact

  • Centralized Amazon performance reporting into a single dashboard.
  • Improved margin focus by making profitability visible at the SKU level.
  • Reduced reporting friction and improved operational alignment.
  • Enabled faster decisions on pricing, inventory, and advertising.

Results

  • Delivered a unified analytics view for sales, margin, and fulfillment.
  • Reduced reporting time and improved decision transparency.
  • Helped prioritize high-margin SKUs and manage low-performing listings.
  • Provided a reliable foundation for future marketplace optimization.

This dashboard helped Amazon marketplace stakeholders move from fragmented reporting to clear, actionable insights that support sales, profitability, and fulfillment decisions.
