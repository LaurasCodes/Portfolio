01 — Challenge

The Problem

The business was treating all customers as a single homogeneous group, applying identical strategies across accounts that had fundamentally different value, behaviour, and growth potential. Financial reporting was similarly flat — monthly summaries that told leadership what had happened, but not why, and not where to focus next.

  • No distinction between high-value and low-value customer accounts
  • Revenue strategy applied uniformly regardless of customer profitability
  • Financial KPIs scattered across spreadsheets with no real-time visibility
  • Leadership had no way to quickly identify which customer groups drove disproportionate revenue
  • Retention and growth efforts were spread thinly rather than targeted at the right segments

Core issue: Without customer segmentation, the business could not prioritise where to invest its time, budget, or retention resources — and without integrated financial reporting, decisions were always made in arrears.

02 — Approach

My Role & Approach

I designed and built an integrated analytics solution combining a customer segmentation model with a real-time financial performance dashboard — giving the business both a clear customer picture and a live view of financial health in one place.

a. Data Preparation

  • Consolidated customer transaction history, account data, and revenue records across all periods
  • Cleaned and standardised customer identifiers, date dimensions, and financial categories
  • Engineered segmentation variables: purchase frequency, average order value, recency, and lifetime value
  • Built calculated financial metrics: gross margin, revenue growth rate, cost-to-serve, and contribution margin

b. Customer Segmentation Model

  • Applied an RFM (Recency, Frequency, Monetary) framework to classify all customers into five distinct segments
  • Validated segment definitions against revenue contribution data to ensure business relevance
  • Mapped segment characteristics to behavioural patterns for strategic targeting

c. Dashboard Development

  • Customer Segment Overview — size, revenue contribution, and growth trajectory of each segment
  • Financial KPI Summary — revenue, margin, cost-to-serve, and growth vs. prior periods
  • Segment-Revenue Bridge — linking specific customer groups directly to financial outcomes
  • Retention Risk Monitor — flagging high-value customers showing declining engagement signals
  • Trend & Forecast View — rolling financial performance and projected revenue by segment

d. Insight Storytelling

I translated both the segmentation model and financial data into clear, prioritised recommendations — framed around where the business should protect, grow, and divest customer relationships.

03 — Insights

Key Insights Uncovered

  1. The top segment (20% of customers) drove 28% of total revenue. These high-value accounts were receiving the same level of attention as low-value ones — a major strategic misalignment that the segmentation model immediately corrected.
  2. A significant share of budget was flowing to low-contribution accounts. The bottom two segments consumed considerable service and marketing resource while delivering minimal revenue — creating a clear reallocation opportunity.
  3. Retention risk was highest among mid-tier customers. A cluster of previously active, mid-value customers showed declining purchase frequency — an early warning signal that targeted retention effort could address before revenue was lost.
  4. Financial margins varied dramatically by segment. High-revenue segments were not always the highest-margin — some accounts required disproportionate cost-to-serve that eroded their apparent profitability.
  5. Flat reporting was masking healthy growth in specific areas. Overall revenue looked flat, but certain segments and product categories were actually growing strongly — obscured by underperforming accounts dragging down the aggregate view.
04 — Impact

Solution Impact

  • Gave leadership a clear, evidence-based view of which customers to prioritise and invest in
  • Financial KPIs became visible in real time — no more waiting for end-of-month spreadsheet compilations
  • Retention efforts were redirected from broad campaigns to specific at-risk, high-value accounts
  • Sales and account management teams aligned their activity to segment priority for the first time
  • Reporting time reduced by 60% as the dashboard replaced multiple disconnected manual processes
  • Strategic planning conversations shifted from "what happened last month" to "what should we do next"
